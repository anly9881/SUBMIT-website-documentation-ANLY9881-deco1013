Sketches

Layout and structure of the website is done through pencil and paper and revised through 2 iterations to improve design decisions. Under each sketch are annotations of design decisions towards why it has been made.

Landing page 1

On a landing page it is important to show the user immediately what they are seeing. The media takes up more space on the website, which becomes salient. There would show AUSDOG's services, donations, stories, and such. This allows the user to quickly flip through and conveniently explore AUSDOG.

The goal of the website is to increase revenue from charity donations. The Donation link is placed on the bottom left below the media. IT has been placed there as users typically read from left to right and would see the donate icon immediately after seeing the cause of what AUSDOG works for in the media slider.

The two bottom right columns are introductory links for the user to learn more about AUSDOG without clicking on the links above for easy access.

Noticably, most websites have their logos situated on the top left and I have adopted that into this layout as users would typically be looking at the top left due to the placement being more uniform amongst websites.

Landing page 2

Compared to landing page 1, this landing page is more populated with information. I have used a grid structure and tried to incorporate an "F" reading style which is typically how a user would scan a webpage. This is by having the 2 lines of the mission statement and a longer reading block for "About us"

I am being considerate with white space on the website. I purposefully made the middle of the webpage to be more spacious to separate the contributions box from the left-hand side of the page. Doing so would attract the user to focus on the left-hand side more where the donations box is situated. It becomes more eye-grabbing. Having the contributions page on the right to support it also gives more incentive to donate whereby users become more aware of what their money would go to and its value.

I have changed the media box into a contributions box. I think that if the website focused more on what AUSDOG has contributed to society more so than marketing their services, it would reinforce the positivity and impact AUSDOG has which in turn would be an incentive for people to donate more rather than seeing its services as people looking for services would have a more direct aim in lodging an application for a support dog.

Social media is a great way to engage its' users to a website. It is highly popular in the digital age we live in. it is important to direct the user to a company's social media quickly and efficiently when a user is looking for it. I have included 3 boxes on top of the page where it can be easily located by the user along with a search bar. Namely Facebook and Instagram are used. The last box would be a donations box as a reassuring second link to remind the user if they would like to donate.

Landing page 3

I have adopted the structure of Landing page 2, however, I have incorporated bigger text boxes and changed the style shape of the tabs. I have rounded the tab shapes and the social media icons to match. I think it would make the tabs more noticeable to use, whereby the shape is very similar to what a normal browser tab looks like. Additionally, I have made spaces between each tab to easily identify each one.

Because the text boxes take a lot of room, it leaves little space for a link to the donations page. It is then placed at the bottom right after seeing marketing/contribution ads. Which in turn is not very effective in placement as it would be the last link to see. Seeing this layout I am not that keen on using this structure.

Landing page 4

Like the other landing page layouts, this layout uses a grid structure. It is important to introduce the company to the user, so the company name is largely placed on the top of the webpage and below it would be an introduction about AUSDOG.

The page options are on the left hand side where the user can easily scroll through what AUSDOG has to offer.

About us page

Introducing the company and its' mission statement is important as users would want instant information. This would also be shown on the landing page. In this wireframe, I have made it as a block text. I do not think it would be relevant to have other information on the page. We would want to immerse the user into knowing what AUSDOG provides and become more aware of its impact on society and be more inclined to donate.

Donation page 1

This page solely focuses on what the user can do to make a difference and be part of the charity. The large box would state reasons why one would donate and the box beside it would be an icon to lead the user to a thank you page after they donate to AUSDOg. The smaller box on the bottom right would aid the reasons why the user should donate to this specific charity and would take them to the contributions page to see how they can also leave an impact.

Donation page 2

After clicking the link to donate on the donation page, they are taken to a thank you page to show company appreciation. It is important to recognise user effort and time to donate to this charity. The large box would be an image with an extended thank you strip. I have placed it this way as being connected would be an easier viewing flow from left to right. At the bottom of the strip is a text box to state what the donation can do and what has been currently achieved reassure the feeling that they have made a difference and have a more lasting positive impact. Either than the strip and this text box, there is no other text on this webpage, this is so it can focus on user appreciation.

Contributions page

The contributions page has an obvious grid structure and using white space for a more organised webpage and attracts the user to each square. Depending on how much the company would like to present, more squares would be used and can be scrolled down to view more media. On the right-hand side margin, I have decided to not place squares there and leave room for a donations section. This section is also noticeably bigger and would be on the side sight as the user browses through the square. This means the user is actively looking at the square without having the intention to.

Designs

After sketching potential pages, low fidelity wireframes have been made..

Wireframes

Landing page

I have combined the landing pages 3 and 2 to make the wireframe. I have adopted the tab design from sketch 3 and the page layout of sketch 2.

I have chosen the layout from sketch 2 as it allows for more information to be presented clearly whilst holding the integrity of the information and most importantly featuring the donate link on the webpage in an attractive spot.

I have chosen the tabs that look more separated rather than connected to make each tab stand out and make it easier for the user to navigate between pages. It is also similar looking to a normal browser tab, and so it can be easily identified. This tab design would be used in the rest of the web pages.

The logos above the tab line are also changed to a circle shape rather than a square. This is to support the design of the rounded tab design and would give off a better aesthetic to the user.

In the wireframe I have also shown what it would look like if a tab was hovered upon. A drop-down menu would ensue. I did not choose the have a sidebar menu as presented on landing page 4 as it would become more cluttered and disorganised due to the website have suboption categories and a collapse feature did not seem appropriate.

About us

I have kept the wireframe very similar to the sketch. The design is neat and organised to present information and continues to have a donation link at the bottom right of the page for increased accessibility for the user to donate.

Donations

This wireframe is the page that gives incentives for individuals to donate. It would show the positive impacts AUSDOG has provided to the community through charity and what the user's donations can impact on other people. This page solely focuses on this aspect and I have not decided to add anything to it and keep other information as separate as possible to not distract the user.

The connected layout of this design is still used like in the sketch. Placing this in a wireframe gives a good representation of how a user would scan the page.

Donations - Thank you

I have kept the integrity of the donations page from the sketch however I have changed the arrow into a box instead to make it more obvious to the user that they would be able to learn more about where their dollar can go to. This would link them to the contributions page.

Contributions

The contributions page is a support webpage to aid the reasons why a user should donate. Showing positive impacts AUSDOG has on the community gives an awareness the user would also be able to make a difference. In the research made. It has been researched positive images and stories would associate the sense of charity and generosity which heightens a person's willingness to donate. Conversely, if negative images were shown to evoke strong empathy it would destimulate the feeling of generosity and would evoke a sense of helplessness that they are not able to change the current situation. Thus having a compilation of stories and or images linked on one page would give reasoning a person should donate to AUSDOG.
